# 4-Week Hip Mobility Program Guide

**Goal:** Improve hip mobility for general health, better squat depth with heels down, and smoother golf rotation without making the routine complicated or time-consuming.

**Time commitment:** 10-12 minutes/day, 5 days/week. Optional 2-minute golf add-on before practice or rounds.

**Important safety notes:** This guide is general fitness education, not medical care. Stop if you feel sharp pain, numbness, tingling, pinching in the front of the hip, or symptoms that linger after the session. Work at a 3-5/10 stretch intensity. Stretching should feel like tension, not pain. Warm up with 2-3 minutes of easy walking or marching before starting.

## Why this plan should help

If your heels come up when you squat, the limitation can come from hips, ankles, trunk control, or a mix of all three. This plan prioritizes hip flexors, adductors, hip internal/external rotation, glutes, and ankles because they are common restrictions for squatting and golf rotation.

## Your 2 quick progress checks

1. **Heels-down squat check:** Once per week, try a comfortable bodyweight squat with feet about shoulder width. Note: How low can you go before the heels lift?
2. **Knee-to-wall ankle check:** Put toes 3-5 inches from a wall and drive the knee toward the wall while keeping the heel down. Note the farthest distance you can reach on each side.

## The daily routine

Do the routine in order. Keep breathing slow. More is not always better; consistency is the goal.

## Four-week progression

| Week | Focus | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | Learn the positions | Do the listed doses. Use support freely. Do not chase depth. |
| Week 2 | Add time under tension | Add 10-15 seconds to stretches #2, #5, and #7 if they feel good. |
| Week 3 | Add control | Slow down #3, #4, and #6. Pause for 2 seconds at the end range of each rep. |
| Week 4 | Integrate squat and golf | After the routine, do 3 easy heels-down squat attempts and 5 slow golf hip turns per side. |

## Form cues and modifications

### 1. 90/90 Breathing Reset
Lie on your back with feet on a wall or chair, knees and hips bent about 90 degrees. Breathe through the nose if possible. Let the ribs soften down as you exhale.

**Make it easier:** Put a pillow under your head or use a couch instead of a wall.

### 2. Half-Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch
Kneel on one knee. Tuck the pelvis slightly, squeeze the glute on the kneeling side, then shift forward just enough to feel the front of that hip.

**Avoid:** Arching the low back to fake range.

### 3. Adductor Rock-Back
Start on hands and knees. Extend one leg out to the side with the foot flat or heel down. Gently rock hips back, then return.

**Make it easier:** Put hands on a bench or couch instead of the floor.

### 4. 90/90 Hip Switches
Sit with both knees bent. Rotate both knees side to side like windshield wipers. Keep the movement slow and controlled.

**Make it easier:** Put both hands behind you. Sit on a folded towel if your back rounds a lot.

### 5. Figure-4 Glute Stretch
Lie on your back. Cross one ankle over the opposite thigh and gently pull the thigh toward you.

**Avoid:** Forcing the knee down. Let the hip open gradually.

### 6. Knee-to-Wall Ankle Rocks
Place one foot a few inches from a wall. Drive the knee toward the wall while keeping the heel down and knee tracking over the middle toes.

**Make it easier:** Start closer to the wall.

### 7. Supported Deep Squat Hold
Hold a door frame, counter, post, or golf cart handle. Squat only as low as you can keep the heels down. Breathe and gently shift side to side.

**Make it easier:** Put a small book under the heels at first, then use less elevation over time.

### 8. Optional Golf Hip Turns
Stand tall with a club or broom across the shoulders. Keep feet planted and rotate hips gently right and left without cranking the low back.

**Golf cue:** Smooth turn first; speed comes later.

## Weekly target

- **Minimum effective dose:** 5 sessions/week.
- **Best time:** After a short walk, workout, shower, or at night when you can be consistent.
- **Expected progress:** You should feel less stiffness in 1-2 weeks. Squat depth usually improves gradually; take the weekly checks seriously.
